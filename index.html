    Heart Diseases

    CHD – Coronary heart disease

    Coronary Heart Disease (CHD), often referred to as the blockage of heart arteries, occurs when the coronary arteries—responsible for supplying blood and oxygen to the heart—become narrowed. This narrowing is usually caused by a buildup of cholesterol, forming plaques on the artery walls. These plaques restrict blood flow, and in severe cases, a blood clot can fully block the artery, potentially leading to serious complications such as a heart attack.

    High Blood Pressure (Hypertension)

    Hypertension is a condition where the force of blood against the artery walls is consistently too high. It's typically defined as blood pressure above 140/90 mmHg. Often called the "silent killer," hypertension can persist for years without noticeable symptoms but significantly increases the risk of heart attacks, strokes, and other serious health issues. Fortunately, it can be easily diagnosed and effectively managed.

    Types of Hypertension

    Primary (essential) hypertension

    For most adults, there’s no identifiable cause of high blood pressure. This type of high blood pressure, called primary (essential) hypertension, tends to develop gradually over many years. In as many as 95% of reported high blood pressure cases, the underlying cause can’t be determined. High blood pressure tends to run in families. Age and race also play a role. Diet and lifestyle also play a big role in essential hypertension.

    This is the most common type, developing gradually over time without a clear cause. Factors contributing to essential hypertension include:

    • Genetics and family history
    • Age and ethnicity
    • Unhealthy diet and sedentary lifestyle
    • Obesity and diabetes
    • Nutritional deficiencies (low potassium, calcium, magnesium)
    • Lack of physical activity
    • Excessive alcohol consumption
    • Chronic stress

     

    Our holistic treatment approach is personalized and aims to identify the underlying causes of hypertension. Treatment plans often include stress management, hormonal balance, detoxification, and lifestyle changes to restore healthy blood pressure levels.

    Diabetes

    Understanding Endocrine Disorders and Diabetes

    Hormonal imbalances can lead to a wide range of health conditions, known as endocrine disorders. Among these, diabetes is the most common and widely recognized.

    Diabetes mellitus, or simply diabetes — refers to a group of conditions that affect how your body converts food into energy. Normally, when you eat carbohydrates, your body turns them into glucose (sugar), which enters your bloodstream. Insulin, a hormone produced by the pancreas, helps your cells absorb this sugar to use for energy.

    Type 2 diabetes two key problems occur:

    1. Your body’s cells become resistant to insulin, meaning they don’t absorb glucose properly.

    2. Your pancreas can’t keep up with the increased demand for insulin.

    This leads to consistently high blood sugar levels, which over time can result in serious complications.

    Because type 2 diabetes can have many causes — including lifestyle, genetics, and environmental factors — it requires more than just medication. A holistic, personalized approach is essential for managing and even reversing the condition.
